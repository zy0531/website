About

Learning in Virtual Environments (LiVE) Lab

Vanderbilt University

Nashville, TN, USA

👋 I'm Yu, but you can also call me Brooke. I'm a computer science PhD candidate at Vanderbilt University advised by Dr. Bobby Bodenheimer. My research lies at the intersection of Virtual & Augmented Reality (VR&AR), Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Eye Tracking, and Spatial Perception & Cognition. In my research, I quantify human spatial perception and cognition in my research to better understand how virtual content in VR&AR affects human behavior and interaction.
I am currently investigating 1️⃣ how to effectively use AR cues to help people with varying abilities understand their spatial environments by conducting experiments in both real-world and simulated virtual environments; and 2️⃣ how gaze patterns and visual attention during spatial learning are related to learning performance. I hope my work will contribute to inform adaptive cognition‑aware AR systems for enhancing spatial learning and decision making. Feel free to reach out at yu.zhao@vanderbilt.edu to share insights and explore new possibilities!

Global-scale Outdoor AR Experience

HoloLens 2 + GNSS

This project's goal is to investigate an outdoor AR navigation experience in the field in order to inform the navigation design in AR googles. People can navigate using existing AR cues (e.g., augmented landmarks) and add virtual markers to help them remember their surroundings.
